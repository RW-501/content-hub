Ozone: Still Zoning or Green Dreams Deferred?

The Ozone Saga: A Glimmer of Hope or Just a Mirage?

In 2025, the ozone layer is showing signs of healing. An MIT-led study confirms that the Antarctic ozone layer is recovering, thanks to global efforts to reduce ozone-depleting substances. The 2024 ozone hole was smaller than in 2023, the smallest since 2020. 

But before we pop the champagne, let's zoom out. The ozone layer is still far from its 1980 levels. Projections suggest recovery by 2066 in the Antarctic and between 2040 and 2045 elsewhere. 

Green Energy: A False Start?

While the ozone layer gets a makeover, global emissions are on the rise. A recent report reveals a rise in U.S. greenhouse gas emissions in early 2025, contributing to a global emissions increase. This trend is linked to a dramatic policy reversal that has slashed the pace of U.S. decarbonization. The rollback of federal incentives, halting of renewable energy projects, and support for fossil fuels has already resulted in over 65,000 clean energy job losses and rising power costs.

The Bottom Line: Is the Ozone Still Zoning?

Yes, the ozone is healing. But global emissions are rising. The 2025 ozone hole is growing and now covers 13 million square kilometers, a little larger than average. Emissions in the first half of 2025 were 0.13% higher than in the first half of 2024.
