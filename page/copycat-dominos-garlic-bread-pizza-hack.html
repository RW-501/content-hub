The Recipe (Straightforward Version)

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of garlic bread (store-bought or homemade)

  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce (jarred or homemade)

  • 1–2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese

  • Toppings of choice (pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, peppers, sausage, etc.)

  • Optional: extra garlic butter for brushing

  • Optional: red pepper flakes, oregano, or parmesan for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Place garlic bread halves cut-side up on a baking sheet.

  3. Spread pizza sauce evenly across the bread.

  4. Sprinkle generously with mozzarella.

  5. Add toppings to your heart’s content.

  6. Bake for 12–15 minutes, until cheese is melted and bubbling.

  7. Optional: brush edges with garlic butter and sprinkle with herbs.

  8. Slice, serve, and surrender to indulgence.

Alternative Ingredients

  • Gluten-free version: Use gluten-free garlic bread or a cauliflower garlic bread base.

  • Low-carb option: Swap garlic bread for zucchini boats brushed with garlic butter.

  • Vegan hack: Use dairy-free cheese and plant-based butter.

  • Gourmet twist: Try roasted garlic focaccia as the base.

  • Breakfast remix: Add scrambled eggs, bacon, and cheddar.

Each swap tells a new story—like the remix album you didn’t know you needed.

How-To (Step by Step for Beginners)

Get your bread right. Garlic bread is your crust now, so pick one that’s firm enough to hold toppings but soft enough to soak in the butter.

Sauce it like you mean it. Don’t skimp—garlic bread has personality, and you need sauce to keep it in check.

Cheese matters. Mozzarella is classic, but mixing in provolone or cheddar gives extra pull.

Top responsibly. Remember: garlic bread is rich. Don’t overload or it collapses under its own delicious weight.

Bake, don’t burn. Keep an eye on it. Garlic butter goes from golden to scorched faster than you can say “Domino’s delivery.”

FAQs

Q1: Is garlic bread strong enough to be a pizza base?

A: Absolutely—if you don’t overload it. Think of it as a flatbread pizza’s rebellious cousin.
Q2: Can I use frozen garlic bread?

A: Yes. Bake it halfway first, then add sauce and toppings before finishing.
Q3: What toppings work best?

A: Classics like pepperoni, mushrooms, and sausage shine, but veggie combos also sing. Avoid overly wet toppings (looking at you, pineapple haters).
Q4: Is this healthier than regular pizza?

A: Let’s be real—it’s garlic bread and pizza. Health was never invited to this party.
