Healthcare Hustle: A City That Cares (and Pays Well)

The Texas Medical Center (TMC) is more than a collection of hospitals—it’s a small city within Houston, employing over 106,000 people and generating billions in annual revenue. From cutting-edge cancer research at MD Anderson to pediatric care at Texas Children’s Hospital, Houston’s healthcare ecosystem is a magnet for top medical talent.

Global Trade: Port City on the Rise

The Port of Houston is the largest port in the U.S. by foreign tonnage, moving everything from petrochemicals to electronics. International trade drives logistics jobs, import/export management, and business strategy roles. Entrepreneurs eye Houston’s trade ecosystem as a launchpad for regional distribution, e-commerce fulfillment, and supply chain innovation.

“Houston isn’t just selling Texas—it’s selling to the world,” says Robert Chang, CEO of a global logistics startup. “The city’s connectivity is unmatched, from rail lines to highways, and yes, to the airport too.”

Culture and Lifestyle: Houston Isn’t All Work

Business isn’t the only thing Houston does well. From the Museum District to the thriving culinary scene (think Tex-Mex, Gulf seafood, and Michelin-starred surprises), the city offers ample opportunities to decompress after a 10-hour hospital shift or a client pitch to an energy conglomerate.

Sports fans, rejoice: Houston is home to the Texans (NFL), Astros (MLB), and Rockets (NBA), while music lovers flock to live venues ranging from indie bars in Montrose to the Houston Symphony.
