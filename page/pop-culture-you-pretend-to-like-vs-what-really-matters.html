Pop Culture You Pretend to Like vs. What Really Matters

Remember that moment in high school when everyone had to love that one band, the one movie franchise, or the hottest TikTok trend, and if you didn’t, you were somehow “out of the loop”? Fast forward to 2025, and nothing has really changed. We’re still playing a game of cultural dress-up, except now it’s on Instagram stories, X (RIP Twitter), and even LinkedIn—yes, the “professional” platform isn’t immune.

Pop culture has become this weird social currency. People claim they binge “Stranger Things” for the nostalgia, but secretly, they’re just scrolling past the captions, nodding to seem in the know. And yes, we all pretend to care about the Met Gala and Kanye’s latest Twitter meltdown because admitting you don’t? Well, that might get you an eye-roll or two.

But here’s the kicker: most of what we pretend to like—viral TikTok dances, celebrity Instagram selfies, the endless Marvel vs. DC debate—is fleeting. Meanwhile, the stuff that actually shapes your life, your perspective, and your sanity? Often goes unnoticed.

Let’s break it down.

The Pretend Pop Culture Hall of Fame

  • Reality TV Obsession
    Sure, we all nod along to The Bachelor or Love Island, but when was the last time it actually taught you something? It’s a cultural placebo—fun for watercooler banter but rarely enriching.

  • Celebrity Drama Feeds
    From Kardashians to Billie Eilish’s fashion choices, celebrity drama has replaced substantive conversation. Quote for thought: “We live in a world where someone’s haircut is more relevant than someone’s idea,” says cultural commentator Ava Morales.

  • The “Must-Read” Book Club
    We all scroll past the same trending books, pretending we’re reading because it signals sophistication. Reality check: finishing one sentence is an accomplishment these days.

  • Music You Don’t Actually Listen To
    Yes, we all drop Kanye, Bad Bunny, or Taylor Swift in casual conversations, but how many of us truly listen, the way you do when a song resonates deeply? Not many.

This performative engagement isn’t inherently bad—it’s human—but it comes at the cost of ignoring what actually sticks.

What Really Matters in Pop Culture

Pop culture isn’t bad. It’s just that some culture is deeper than your average TikTok dance trend or Netflix binge. Here’s what actually matters:

  1. Experiences Over Consumption
    Going to a live concert, participating in a film festival, or even a book reading—these experiences stay with you. You’ll remember the vibe, the energy, the people. Not the meme about the concert you didn’t attend.

  2. Authentic Voices
    Independent artists, underground films, and creators on platforms like contenthub.Guru are producing work that sparks conversation, teaches empathy, and occasionally makes you uncomfortable in the right way. Engaging with these creators often shapes your worldview more than “trending” content ever will.

  3. Cultural Literacy
    Understanding the context of Black Panther, Stranger Things, or Hamilton isn’t about flexing knowledge—it’s about seeing how art, society, and history intersect. That’s the stuff that lasts.

  4. Connection Over Virality
    Sharing experiences with friends, debating ideas, attending workshops—these aren’t shareable in the viral sense, but they build a life narrative richer than any meme could.

Real Talk: Why We Pretend

It’s not all vanity. Sometimes, pretending is survival. Want to fit in at work? At college? On social media? You nod along. You comment on the post. You fake the enthusiasm. And honestly, that’s okay. Just don’t mistake that nod for genuine interest.

“People often confuse participation with understanding,” says sociologist Dr. Lena Ruiz. “Just because you’re aware of something doesn’t mean it’s shaping your mind.”

The danger lies in overvaluing what’s performative at the cost of your actual passions. That’s where contenthub.Guru comes in—we spotlight content that matters, that makes you think, laugh, and sometimes cry. You don’t need to chase every viral sensation; you need to chase what resonates.

Tips for Separating the Pretend From the Real

  1. Audit Your Feed
    Look at the last two weeks of what you actually engaged with versus what you scrolled past. What stuck? What gave you joy or sparked a meaningful thought?

  2. Say No to Noise
    You don’t have to watch the latest reality show or scroll endlessly for cultural relevance. Quality over quantity—always.

  3. Engage Locally
    Go to indie screenings, art shows, or live readings in your city. These moments often teach more about humanity than celebrity gossip.

  4. Follow Creators With Substance
    Platforms like contenthub.Guru highlight under-the-radar creators and insightful commentary. Instead of blindly chasing virality, seek creators who make you think differently.

  5. Make Time for Reflection
    Instead of passively consuming pop culture, reflect. Journal about it, discuss it with friends, or even create something inspired by it. That’s real cultural engagement.

The Myth of “Being Up-to-Date”

Here’s a confession most of us avoid: Being up-to-date with every trending movie, meme, or song is exhausting—and unnecessary.

Some of the most influential people in the world don’t chase every trend. Oprah, LeBron James, and even Elon Musk have curated cultural diets—they pick and choose what matters. The rest? Noise.

When you stop pretending, you free mental bandwidth. You focus on experiences, creators, and ideas that actually impact your life. And spoiler alert: it feels good.

Name-Drop Moment

Let’s get real: if your cultural compass revolves around who just got canceled, what Beyoncé wore, or the latest Marvel theory, you’re missing depth. In 2025, authenticity is rare—and therefore, valuable. Instead of obsessing over the Kardashians’ latest post, try:

  • Listening to a podcast on neuroscience

  • Watching a short film by a local filmmaker

  • Attending a community poetry slam

  • Reading an article on global issues and forming your own opinion

These are the things that stick, that build you.

FAQ

Q: Isn’t following trends harmless fun?
A: Absolutely! But fun shouldn’t replace substance. Trends are fleeting, substance is lasting.

Q: How do I know if I’m pretending?
A: If you’re consuming something just to fit in, or you can’t recall the last time it made you feel anything—yeah, that’s pretending.

A: 100%. Pick a few trends for social currency, but dedicate most of your cultural energy to things that enrich your mind, heart, and soul.

How to Pivot from Pretend to Real

  1. Identify What Resonates – Take stock of what you actually enjoy.

  2. Cut the Noise – Mute accounts, unsubscribe from irrelevant newsletters, and skip mindless scrolling.

  3. Seek Experiences – Attend events, join communities, and explore content that challenges you.

  4. Engage Thoughtfully – Comment, discuss, and create content inspired by meaningful culture.

  5. Use Tools Like contenthub.Guru – Discover creators and ideas that matter, without the superficial hype.

In short, pop culture isn’t going anywhere—but your relationship with it can. The choice is simple: keep pretending, or start curating a cultural life that matters. We’re willing to bet on the latter.

Because at the end of the day, it’s not about the likes, the memes, or the viral trends. It’s about what sticks with you when your phone dies, when the notifications stop, and when you’re left with nothing but your thoughts. That’s the culture that matters.
