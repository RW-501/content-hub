The Shortlist: 2025’s Top Cities for Travel Creators

1. Lisbon, Portugal

  • Internet: Avg 200–250 Mbps (fiber internet widely available)

  • Scenery: Alfama alleys, coastal cliffs, pastel sunsets.

  • Safety: Ranked among Europe’s safest capitals.

  • Budget: Cheaper than Paris, more soulful than Madrid.

Lisbon is basically Europe’s cheat code for creators. You get sun 300 days a year, insanely photogenic neighborhoods, and a city that feels like an artist residency with WiFi. Creators are flocking here like pigeons to pasteis de nata.

2. Chiang Mai, Thailand

  • Internet: 250–300 Mbps in cafes and co-working spaces.

  • Scenery: Temples, mountains, night bazaars.

  • Safety: Friendly, low-crime city.

  • Budget: $800/month can cover rent, food, and your latte addiction.

The O.G. digital nomad capital still holds the crown. Chiang Mai is the kind of place where you can film a temple sunrise at 6 a.m. and edit it poolside by 10 a.m. It’s cheap, it’s creative, and the food? Forget it—you’ll never eat an $18 avocado toast again.

3. Medellín, Colombia

  • Internet: 200 Mbps in many neighborhoods.

  • Scenery: Mountain city, lush greenery, vibrant street art.

  • Safety: Safer than its old reputation, but neighborhoods matter.

  • Budget: Comfortable living under $1,200/month.

Medellín is proof that reinvention is possible. Once infamous, now innovative. Creators rave about the city’s energy—rooftop views, cable cars, and endless murals. Just pick the right barrio (El Poblado or Laureles) and you’re golden.

“Medellín turned my content into a vibe. It’s colorful “Medellín turned my content into a vibe. It’s colorful chaos, but safe enough to thrive.” – Lena, travel vlogger from Berlin

4. Tallinn, Estonia

  • Internet: Some of the fastest in Europe (300+ Mbps).

  • Scenery: Medieval meets modern—cobblestone next to co-working hubs.

  • Safety: Ultra-safe, one of the least corrupt countries globally.

  • Budget: Higher than Asia, lower than Western Europe.

Tallinn is the underdog creators didn’t know they needed. A digital-first country (you can literally e-vote), this city is where tech geeks and fairytale dreamers collide. One minute you’re filming a gothic church, the next you’re Zooming with your editor on WiFi faster than your brain can process.

5. Mexico City, Mexico

  • Internet: Stronger than ever—200 Mbps in hotspots.

  • Scenery: Street food stalls, Aztec ruins, colorful markets.

  • Safety: Safer in central neighborhoods (Roma, Condesa).

  • Budget: Affordable food, trendy Airbnbs.

CDMX is a sensory overload in the best way. Tacos al pastor dripping down your wrist, lucha libre nights, rooftop mezcal bars—it’s all content gold. Honorable Mentions

  • Seoul, South Korea – Blazing WiFi, neon-lit streets, endless K-culture pull.

  • Tbilisi, Georgia – Budget-friendly, funky architecture, rising creator scene.

  • Cape Town, South Africa – Jaw-dropping landscapes, but safety requires vigilance.

The Culture Clash: Creator vs. Local

Let’s get honest. Creators often change the cities they land in. Sometimes for better, sometimes not. Rent spikes in Lisbon? Blame a wave of creators. Overflowing Bali rice fields with influencers doing “yoga for the ‘Gram”? Same deal.

So if you’re going to film in these cities, remember: respect the locals. Buy local. Tip generously. Don’t be the stereotype.

How to Choose Your Creator City

Speed First: Always test WiFi before signing a lease. Ask for screenshots of speed tests.

Safety Check: Research neighborhoods, not just cities. (CDMX Roma ≠ outskirts at night.)

Scenery Bank: Can you film 20 different backdrops within 10 miles? If yes, jackpot.

Budget Reality: How to Choose Your Creator City,000 stretches far in Chiang Mai, but barely in Seoul.

Community Factor: Find your tribe. Co-working spaces, meetups, and creator groups matter.

Real Talk Tips

  • Always carry backup data. Portable WiFi or eSIMs like Airalo are game-changers.

  • Film early. 6 a.m. gives you better light and less risk.

  • Gear insurance is sexy. Don’t skip it—stolen cameras = broken careers.

FAQ

Q1: What city is best overall for travel creators?

A: Lisbon is the sweet spot—fast internet, safety, and scenery. But if budget is king, Chiang Mai wins.
Q2: Which city has the fastest internet?

A: Seoul, South Korea, often hits 400 Mbps+—upload paradise.
Q3: What about U.S. cities?

A: Austin, Miami, and Portland are solid, but internet is pricier and safety varies. Global cities often give you more bang for your buck.
Q4: How do I stay safe filming outside?

A: Film with friends, use smaller rigs when possible, and scout neighborhoods in daylight first.
