What You’ll Need (a.k.a. The Dream Team of Ingredients)

Here’s the classic lineup for Buffalo Chicken Flatbread Pizza:

  • Cooked chicken: Rotisserie chicken is your BFF. Shred it like you’re auditioning for a Food Network cameo.

  • Buffalo sauce: Frank’s RedHot if you’re a purist. Any hot sauce works if you’re feeling rebellious.

  • Mozzarella cheese: Because pizza without mozzarella is just sadness on carbs.

  • Blue cheese crumbles: Controversial, but deeply rewarding if you’re a fan.

  • Ranch dressing: For drizzling. Don’t argue—just do it.

  • Green onions or celery: Fresh crunch = chef’s kiss.

Ingredient Swaps & Alternatives

  • Flatbread substitute: Tortillas (for a thinner, crispier vibe) or pita bread.

  • Chicken substitute: Cauliflower florets for a vegetarian version. Yes, cauliflower can be fun.

  • Cheese swap: Swap mozzarella for provolone, gouda, or pepper jack if you want smoky, melty drama.

  • Buffalo sauce alternative: Mix hot sauce with honey for a sweet-heat combo.

The Recipe (Write This Down, or Screenshot It for Posterity)

Buffalo Chicken Flatbread Pizza (Better Than Pizza Hut!)

Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 12 minutes
Serves: 2–3 hungry humans

Ingredients:

  • 2 flatbreads (naan, pita, or tortillas)

  • 1 ½ cups cooked shredded chicken

  • ⅓ cup buffalo sauce

  • 1 ½ cups shredded mozzarella cheese

  • ¼ cup blue cheese crumbles (optional)

  • 2 tbsp ranch dressing (for drizzling)

  • 2 tbsp chopped green onions or celery (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place flatbreads on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  2. In a bowl, toss shredded chicken with buffalo sauce until fully coated.

  3. Spread chicken evenly over flatbreads. Top with mozzarella and a sprinkle of blue cheese (if using).

  4. Bake for 10–12 minutes, or until cheese is bubbling and golden.

  5. Remove from oven. Drizzle with ranch, scatter green onions/celery, and slice like the hero you are.

How-To: The Foolproof Method

Sauce the chicken, not the bread. This keeps the flatbread crisp instead of soggy.

Cheese goes on top like a blanket. Don’t skimp—this is the glue that holds society (and your pizza) together.

High heat, short time. Flatbreads cook quickly. Don’t wander off to check TikTok.

Finish with drama. The ranch drizzle is your Broadway finale.

FAQ – Because You’ll Ask

Q1: Can I make this ahead of time?

A: Yes, assemble the flatbread and refrigerate. Just add 2–3 extra minutes to the bake time.
Q2: I hate blue cheese. Do I have to use it?

A: Absolutely not. Use feta, cheddar, or more mozzarella. You’re the boss of your pizza.
Q3: Can I make it spicy or mild?

A: Totally. Use extra hot buffalo sauce for fire, or a honey-hot mix for a gentler bite.
Q4: What side dishes go well with it?

A: Keep it light: celery sticks, carrot sticks, or a crisp salad. Or don’t, and just eat two flatbreads.
