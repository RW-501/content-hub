Myth 1: ‚ÄúThe Twin Towers Were Brought Down by Controlled Demolition‚Äù

Perhaps the most persistent 9/11 myth is that the Twin Towers fell because of an intentional demolition rather than the planes crashing into them.

Fact:
Extensive investigations by organizations including the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) concluded that the towers collapsed due to the impact of the planes and the subsequent fires. Jet fuel ignited office materials, weakening the steel structure over time. The collapse was a tragic consequence of fire-induced structural failure, not explosives planted within the building.

Why do myths like this persist? Traumatic events often trigger suspicion and the need for explanations that feel more ‚Äúdeliberate‚Äù than sheer randomness. But the facts are well-documented, with eyewitness testimony, video evidence, and forensic analysis confirming the cause.

Myth 2: ‚ÄúNo Plane Hit the Pentagon‚Äù

Some conspiracy theories claim the Pentagon was hit by a missile rather than American Airlines Flight 77.

Fact:
Flight 77, a Boeing 757, struck the Pentagon, killing 125 people inside the building and all 64 passengers and crew on the plane. Photographic evidence, radar data, and eyewitness accounts clearly document the impact. The missile theory ignores the large airplane debris found at the scene and the coroner reports of plane passengers.

It‚Äôs worth noting that trauma and shock often make people question the impossible. But careful investigation consistently supports the facts.

Myth 3: ‚ÄúFlight 93 Was Shot Down‚Äù

Another myth involves United Flight 93, which crashed in a Pennsylvania field after passengers fought the hijackers. Some claim the government shot it down.

Fact:
Flight 93 crashed after passengers stormed the cockpit in an attempt to retake control. The cockpit voice recorder and passenger phone calls confirm their heroic efforts. There is no credible evidence supporting the idea that the plane was shot down.

This myth can overshadow the bravery of ordinary citizens who made extraordinary choices. Honoring their memory requires understanding the truth: they prevented an even larger catastrophe.

Myth 4: ‚ÄúEveryone in the Towers Survived‚Äù

Some misinformation online suggests that certain populations survived or were warned in advance.

Fact:
Tragically, 2,753 people died in the attacks on the World Trade Center, including first responders who rushed in to save lives. While some were able to escape, the scale of the loss was immense, and no verified warnings were given that allowed widespread evacuation before the attacks.

It is essential to confront these facts respectfully to honor the victims and their families.

How to Verify 9/11 Facts

Check Official Reports: NIST, the 9/11 Commission Report, and FAA investigations are reliable sources.

Evaluate Sources: Avoid social media claims without corroboration from credible news organizations or official records.

Look for Multiple Confirmations: Eyewitness accounts, video evidence, and forensic analysis should align.

Consider Motivations: Ask why a particular story or theory exists‚Äîdoes it have political or financial motivation?

Understanding Why Myths Spread

Human psychology plays a role. We try to make sense of events that are overwhelming, random, or horrific. The 9/11 attacks were unprecedented, and questions like ‚Äúhow could this happen?‚Äù are natural. However, myths that assign blame or suggest secret plots can distort history and dishonor the victims.
