Pizza Hut Copycat Pan Pizza Recipe – Make the Iconic Thick Crust at Home

There are two kinds of people in this world:

  1. Those who swear by thin, crisp Neapolitan crusts that bubble and char like some kind of edible parchment.

  2. And those who want a pizza that feels like a hug from carbs—thick, buttery, crunchy-edged, and unapologetically American.

If you’re reading this, congratulations. You are very likely in the second camp. (Or you are in the first camp but secretly cheat on your sourdough with Pizza Hut when no one is watching.)

Yes, we are talking about the legendary Pan Pizza from Pizza Hut—that dense yet airy, golden, fried-on-the-bottom dough that changed American pizza night forever.

The best part? You don’t need a corporate pizza oven or a franchise license to recreate it. You just need a cast-iron skillet, some patience, and the willingness to eat three slices before you even think about serving anyone else.

And here at ContentHub.Guru, where we break down ideas into digestible bites (pun fully intended), we’re serving you not just the recipe—but the culture, the how-to, the frequently asked “Why is my dough so sad?” questions, and a side of wit.

So grab your apron. This is going to be tasty.

Why Pizza Hut’s Pan Pizza Became Iconic

In 1980, Pizza Hut rolled out its Pan Pizza to the world. Unlike the thinner hand-tossed varieties, this one was cooked in a deep, oil-slicked pan that gave it a crispy fried edge and an interior that was part breadstick, part cloud.

It wasn’t pizza. It was an event. Every bite was a sensory contrast: crunch on the outside, soft chew in the middle, cheese that stretched like a sitcom gag, and tomato sauce that carried just enough sweetness.

At home, we’ve tried to chase that dragon—making deep-dish pizzas, focaccia pizzas, and yes, those awkward college attempts where we used too much yeast and wound up with pizza that looked like a puffy pillow.

But today, we’re going to do it right.

The Secret to Copycat Pizza Hut Pan Pizza

The secrets are surprisingly simple:

  1. The Pan: Cast-iron or a well-oiled cake pan. Pizza Hut used steel pans seasoned with oil for that fried base.

  2. The Oil: Don’t skimp. The dough literally fries in it while baking. Olive oil is fine, but if you want authenticity—vegetable or canola oil mimics the chain’s flavor.

  3. The Dough: High-gluten or bread flour gives that chewy, bready texture.

  4. The Sauce: Slightly sweet, lightly herby.

  5. The Cheese: A blend of mozzarella with a whisper of provolone or Monterey Jack.

Ingredients (Standard Version)

For two 9-inch pizzas (because one is never enough):

Dough

  • 2 ½ cups bread flour (or all-purpose in a pinch)

  • 1 tsp salt

  • 1 tbsp sugar

  • 2 tsp instant yeast

  • 1 cup warm water (110°F)

  • 2 tbsp olive oil (plus more for pans)

Sauce

  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce

  • 1 tsp sugar

  • 1 tsp dried oregano

  • ½ tsp garlic powder

  • ½ tsp onion powder

  • ½ tsp salt

  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Toppings

  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella

  • Optional: ½ cup provolone or Monterey Jack

  • Pepperoni, veggies, or whatever your guilty pleasure is

Alternative Ingredients (For the Adventurous or Gluten-Free Crowd)

  • Flour Swap: Use 1:1 gluten-free flour mix plus 1 tsp xanthan gum if gluten is your nemesis.

  • Oil Swap: Swap vegetable oil with ghee or avocado oil for a richer crust.

  • Cheese Swap: Try smoked mozzarella or a vegan cheese blend.

  • Sauce Swap: Blend roasted red peppers and tomatoes for a smoky twist.

Remember, pizza is democracy in a pan. The topping ballot is always open.

Step-by-Step: How to Cook Pizza Hut Copycat Pan Pizza

  1. Proof the Yeast (3 min): In a mixing bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Wait until it foams like a science fair volcano.

  2. Mix the Dough (5 min): Add flour, salt, and olive oil. Stir until it looks shaggy. Knead for 8 minutes until smooth.

  3. First Rise (1 hour): Place dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it double in size.

  4. Prepare Pans (5 min): Generously oil your cast-iron skillet or cake pans—like, pour 3 tbsp oil in each. You want shallow puddles.

  5. Press Dough (10 min): Divide dough in half, place each in a pan, and gently press out to edges. Cover and let rise 30–40 minutes until puffy.

  6. Preheat Oven: Set it to 450°F (pizza craves high heat).

  7. Add Sauce & Cheese (5 min): Spread sauce, then add your cheese blanket and toppings.

  8. Bake (15–20 min): Until crust is golden and cheese is bubbling like molten lava.

  9. Cool Slightly: Let sit 3 minutes (yes, torture) before slicing into cheesy squares.

The Recipe Card (For Printing, Saving, Tattooing)

Pizza Hut Copycat Pan Pizza
Servings: 2 Pizzas | Time: 2 hrs (mostly rising time)

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups bread flour

  • 1 cup warm water

  • 2 tsp yeast

  • 1 tbsp sugar

  • 1 tsp salt

  • 2 tbsp olive oil + oil for pans

  • 2 cups mozzarella, toppings

Instructions:

  1. Proof yeast.

  2. Mix & knead dough.

  3. Let rise 1 hr.

  4. Oil pans generously.

  5. Press dough in pans, let rise again.

  6. Preheat oven 450°F.

  7. Add sauce, cheese, toppings.

  8. Bake 15–20 min.

  9. Rest 3 min. Slice & serve.

FAQs

Q1: Do I need a cast-iron skillet?

A: No, but it’s best. A heavy 9-inch cake pan works fine.
Q2: Why so much oil?

A: That’s the secret. The crust fries in oil, which is why it’s different from standard pizza.
Q3: Can I make the dough ahead?

A: Absolutely. Let it slow-rise in the fridge overnight for even more flavor.
Q4: My crust is soggy—help!

A: Bake on the lower oven rack. That heat blast crisps the bottom.

How-To: Elevate Your Copycat Pizza

For Extra Crust Crunch: Sprinkle cornmeal in the pan before adding dough.

For Stringy Cheese Pulls: Add a sprinkle of provolone.

For That Pizza Hut Nostalgia: Pair it with root beer in a red plastic cup.
